About Vinyasa Flow

Vinyasa flow is a dynamic style of yoga that synchronizes breath with movement, creating a continuous, flowing sequence of poses, often known as "flow" or "moving meditation". It emphasizes smooth transitions, primarily using Sun Salutations to build heat, strength, and flexibility, with no two classes usually the same.

About Power Yoga

Power Yoga is a dynamic, fitness-based Vinyasa style developed in the 1990s from Ashtanga Yoga to build strength, flexibility, and endurance. It features fast-paced sequences, intense poses, and high-intensity, "one breath, one movement" flow. It is designed to be accessible to all levels, with modifications for beginners and intense variations for advanced practitioners.

About Yin Yoga

Yin Yoga is a slow-paced, meditative style involving passive poses held for 3–7 minutes, targeting deep connective tissues (fascia, ligaments, joints) rather than muscles. Primarily targeting the hips, pelvis, and lower spine, this practice improves flexibility, enhances joint mobility, and promotes deep relaxation, making it a perfect complement to active "Yang" workouts.

About Hot Yoga

Hot yoga is a method of practicing yoga in an intentionally heated room, typically between 35–40.5°C, designed to intensify the workout, increase flexibility, and promote sweating. It can encompass various styles—such as Bikram or Vinyasa—that are performed in high temperatures.
